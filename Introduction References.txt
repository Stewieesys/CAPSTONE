1. First Paragraph
Forests are among the most valuable natural resources on Earth because they provide numerous environmental, economic, and social benefits. They serve as habitats for diverse species of plants and animals, helping maintain biodiversity and ecological balance. Forests also play a significant role in regulating climate by absorbing carbon dioxide and producing oxygen. In addition, they help protect watersheds, prevent soil erosion, and reduce the risk of natural disasters. Many communities depend on forests for food, livelihood, medicine, and other essential resources. As a result, the conservation and protection of forests are important for both present and future generations.

Despite their importance, forests are increasingly threatened by various illegal and destructive activities. Illegal logging remains one of the most common causes of forest degradation and deforestation in many countries. Unauthorized land clearing and forest encroachment also contribute to the loss of forest cover and wildlife habitats. These activities disrupt ecosystems and negatively affect the plants and animals that depend on forests for survival. Furthermore, human activities can increase the likelihood of forest fires and other environmental hazards. If left unchecked, these threats can cause long-term damage to the environment and natural resources.

One of the major concerns in forest conservation is the lack of efficient forest management and monitoring systems. Many forest areas are vast, remote, and difficult to access, making regular inspections challenging for authorities and environmental agencies. Traditional monitoring methods rely heavily on manual patrols, field inspections, and reports from local communities to identify environmental threats and illegal activities. These approaches often require significant time, effort, and financial resources, limiting the ability of personnel to conduct continuous observation across large forest areas. As a result, incidents such as illegal logging, forest encroachment, and early signs of fire may remain undetected for extended periods, leading to delayed response and increased environmental damage. Therefore, there is a growing need for more advanced monitoring solutions that can provide real-time information and support sustainable forest management efforts.

Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 13, known as Climate Action, focuses on taking urgent measures to combat climate change and its impacts. It aims to strengthen resilience and adaptive capacity to climate-related hazards and natural disasters in all countries. The goal emphasizes the importance of integrating climate change measures into policies, strategies, and planning processes. It also promotes education, awareness, and capacity-building efforts related to climate change mitigation and adaptation. Meanwhile, Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 15, known as Life on Land, focuses on protecting, restoring, and promoting the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems. It aims to sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, halt biodiversity loss, and ensure the conservation of natural habitats for future generations.
